How it compares

Smallpdf and iLovePDF offer similar conversion but require signup after a few uses and upload your images. Freeimagetopdf has no usage limits and processes everything locally. For OCR on scanned images, dedicated services like Smallpdf or Adobe Acrobat are better. For pure image-to-PDF conversion, Freeimagetopdf is simpler and free.

Privacy

Images stay in your browser. Conversion happens client-side with no server upload.

Frequently asked questions

Is Freeimagetopdf free with no limits?

Yes. No daily limits, no signup, no watermarks.

What image formats does it accept?

JPG, PNG, WebP, and most browser-supported image formats.

Can I change the order of pages?

Yes. Drag images into any order before conversion.

Is image quality preserved?

Yes. Images are embedded at original quality. For smaller file sizes, compress images first with Freeimagecompressor.

Are images uploaded to a server?

No. All processing is client-side in your browser.

How many images can I combine?

No artificial limit. Bounded by browser memory — typically dozens to hundreds of images.
