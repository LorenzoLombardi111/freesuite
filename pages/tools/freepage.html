Free Document Writer — rich-text editing without the Google account

A full rich-text document editor in your browser. Headings, bold and italic, lists, links, images, tables. Exports to DOCX and PDF. No signup.

Features

  • Rich-text editor with formatting toolbar
  • Headings (H1-H6), bold, italic, underline, strikethrough
  • Bulleted and numbered lists, blockquotes
  • Insert links, images, and tables
  • Export to DOCX (Word-compatible) or PDF
  • Autosave to browser local storage
  • Dark mode, works offline after first load

How it works

  1. Open freepage.app
  2. Start writing with full formatting controls in the toolbar
  3. Document autosaves as you type
  4. Export to DOCX or PDF when ready to share

Common use cases

  • Writing cover letters and resumes
  • Drafting formatted reports
  • Creating quick proposals
  • Writing blog post drafts with formatting
  • Academic paper drafts

How it compares

Compared to Google Docs, Freepage has no account requirement and no cloud sync — your documents stay on your device. For team collaboration, Google Docs is still the right choice. For individual formatted writing, Freepage is faster to start and more private.

Privacy

Documents live in your browser's local storage. Export to DOCX or PDF for permanent keeping.

Frequently asked questions

Is Freepage really free?

Yes. Completely free, no signup, no paid tier, no ads, no watermarks.

Can I export to Microsoft Word format?

Yes. Freepage exports to .docx which opens in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice, and Apple Pages.

Is there real-time collaboration?

No. Freepage is designed for individual writing without the complexity of shared editing. For team collaboration, use Google Docs.

Can I paste content from Word or Google Docs?

Yes. Paste from other sources and formatting is preserved where possible.

Does Freepage work offline?

Yes. After the first page load, Freepage works without an internet connection as a Progressive Web App.

Where are my documents saved?

In your browser's local storage by default. Use the export button to download a .docx or .pdf copy to your device.
